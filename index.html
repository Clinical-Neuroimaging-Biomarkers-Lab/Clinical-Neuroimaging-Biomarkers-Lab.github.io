Clinical Neuroimaging & Biomarkers Lab

University of Queensland

Our mission is to pioneer new approaches for understanding how trauma shapes the brain and body, and to translate these insights into more effective and accessible interventions. We integrate advanced neuroimaging, large-scale biobanking, and computational modelling with lived-experience co-design to identify mechanisms of vulnerability and resilience, discover biomarkers, and inform precision therapeutics.

By bridging basic and translational neuroscience with next-generation technologies, including AI-enabled analysis, data-sharing initiatives, and open science, we aim to transform how trauma-related mental illnesses are understood, diagnosed, and treated.

We develop and evaluate innovative therapeutics, including psychedelic-assisted psychotherapy and precision rehabilitation approaches, in partnership with clinicians, industry, and lived-experience communities. Our research also extends to related and comorbid mental illnesses such as depression, and leverages models of brain injury (e.g., stroke and traumatic brain injury) to investigate how biological trauma contributes to psychopathology and recovery.

Dr. Lena Oestreich

I am a biological psychologist and computational neuroscientist with a PhD in clinical psychology from the University of New South Wales, and postdoctoral training in clinical trials and computational psychiatry at the University of Queensland. My research focuses on how trauma shapes the brain and body across the lifespan, and how these insights can be translated into more effective and accessible treatments for trauma-related mental illnesses.

I currently hold a joint appointment as Group Leader at the Australian Institute for Bioengineering and Nanotechnology and the School of Psychology at the University of Queensland, where I lead the Clinical Neuroimaging and Biomarkers Lab. I am committed to open science, equitable participation in research, and advancing interdisciplinary approaches that bridge neuroscience, clinical practice, and technological innovation.
